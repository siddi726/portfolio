Bio

I am currently completing my fourth undergraduate year in Material Science Engineering at the University of Toronto. I am also pursuing an Engineering Business Minor. My interests are in data analytics and the automotive industry.

Throughout my time in University, I tried a variety of experiences ranging from research to non-engineering work. Each of those experiences let me gain valuable skills and gave me better insight of what I want to do in the future.

This past year, I was given the opportunity to develop a free and accessible design that that evaluates and suggests safer cleaning and disinfection products. The project allowed me to hone my leadership and communication skills while also deepening my interest in working with data. See more details regarding the project here.

Work Experiences

BMW ConnectedDrive

I had the opportunity to work for BMW Group Canada under the ConnectedDrive Team. I utilized BMW webservices and software to resolve technology issues inside BMW vehicles, typically within 24 hours. By collaborating with internal teams and retailers I was able to reduce the number of recurring cases found in vehicles by 60%. I also managed the expextations of 400 clients during a service discontinuation campaign by acting as a liason between them and the engineering team.

Throughout my intership, I was able to develop valueable data management and client communication skills. It also gave me a greater appreciation towards the importance of data analytics in the automotive industry.

Markdom Plastic Products

As a warehouse team member at Markdom Plastic Products, organized pallets of car parts and optimized the storage spaces in the warehouse on top of preparing for scheduled shipments. By doing this, I helped increase the overall morale and effeciency of the warehouse. I also trained two new employees within a week near the end of my work term.

At Markdom, I was able to improve my time management and organizational skills. With my work being mainly independent, I also learned the importance of self-management and knowing when to ask for help when necessary.

Design Experiences

Alternative Materials Discovery - ChemEval

For my 4th year Capstone Project, our team created ChemEval.ca. It is a free website that that evaluates and suggests safer cleaning and disinfection products in a much faster and accessible manner compared to existing alternatives.

My main responsibilities were to lead team and client meetings, delegate tasks, and manage the team dynamic. Overall, I made sure all of our members were on the same page and encouraged an environment where people can pitch their ideas freely. My technical responsibilities revolved around developing the initial database that would store the information on various cleaning and disinfection products. By the end of the project, the database had accrued over 100 unique products.

This capstone project was a great opportunity to hone my leadership skills and work with engineers with different technical backgrounds.

UofT Design League - VP of Educational Technology

As VP of Educational Technology at the UofT Design League, I oversaw workshops that taught students about CAD design and simulation. I also managed the minature competitions we held throught the year and led my team to create the design questions used at our annual Designathon.

I frequently collaborated with other teams inside and outside of the club to bring fresh and exiciting events to students. For example, I worked with an architecture club at UofT to host a joint engineering/architecture competiton - UofTransit. I also pushed for an incentives system to be added to our workshops, which increased participation by 40%.

Research Experiences

Surface Engineering of CNT Electrodes

At the Flexible Energy and Electronics Lab (F.E.E.L) at UofT, researched and developed a near-ideal composite by synthesizing and evaluating the capacitive behavior of coated Carbon Nanotube (CNT) samples. My work included preparing and testing coated CNT samples as well as analyzing the relative effectiveness of different coated layers.

Apart from my typical lab duties, I also helped improve the efficiency of sample preparation by utilizing unused machinery to quickly create the thin CNT samples used for testing. With the new method, I was able to prepare the same amount of samples about 10 times faster.

By the end of my reseach term, I had presented my findings at a UofT undergraduate research conference and was listed as a co-author in 2 research papers.
